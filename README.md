## What Separates a Good UAE Proxy From a Bad One

Most providers will tell you they offer global coverage. The actual experience varies wildly. Here's what to actually check before you swipe a card:

- **IP pool size and freshness.** A pool that hasn't been refreshed in months is a pool full of already-flagged IPs. Useless for scraping.
- **Protocol support.** HTTP, HTTPS, and SOCKS5. If a provider only offers HTTP, walk away.
- **Authentication options.** Username/password and IP whitelist should both be available.
- **Bandwidth model.** Some providers charge per GB. Others charge per proxy port. The right model depends on your traffic pattern.
- **Concurrent threads.** If you're scraping at scale, hard caps on simultaneous connections will kill your throughput.
- **Replacement policy.** Bad IPs happen. The question is whether the provider lets you swap them out without a fight.

A quick plain-language summary: the cheapest proxy is rarely the best deal. The fastest proxy is sometimes overkill. Match the proxy type to the actual job.
